Find and Shutdown Frosty's Snowglobe Machine

Difficulty:
Location: Data Center Corridors

Objective: You've heard murmurings around the city about a wise, elderly gnome having a change of heart. He must have information about where Frosty's Snowglobe Machine is. You should find and talk to the gnome so you can get some help with how to make your way through the Data Center's labrynthian halls. Once you find the Snowglobe Machine, figure out how to shut it down and melt Frosty's cold, nefarious plans.

Working through the Challenge:
Write-up notes

The Elder Gnome can be found just outside the train station. Interacting with him reveals the dialog shown above. He mentions a code outside the building, and while walking around the perimeter of the data center, we see some interesting-looking brickwork.

Unusual brick pattern along the lower exterior wall
Write-up notes

Some bricks along the lower wall stand out with a distinct dark/light pattern. There are six rows, each row containing eight bricks. If we treat each row as an 8-bit binary value (dark = 0, light = 1), yielding six bytes, decoding those bytes as ASCII reveals a short path hint left behind by employees:

  • 01101001 = 0x69 = i
  • 01101101 = 0x6D = m
  • 01100001 = 0x61 = a
  • 01101110 = 0x6E = n
  • 01101111 = 0x6F = o
  • 01001011 = 0x4B = K

Together: imanoK

imanoK is Konami spelled backwards — a clear nod to the infamous video game cheat code. I don’t think it will get us 30 free lives, but it’s worth noting. We’ll keep the code (in reverse) in mind for later while navigating the maze.

Konami code (backwards):

A B ➡️ ⬅️ ➡️ ⬅️ ⬇️ ⬇️ ⬆️ ⬆️

Next we'll explore the Data Center corridors. When we first enter the corridors, it's dark and a bit hard to see...

Entering the Data Center corridors: it’s dark...
Write-up notes

The path isn’t too hard to navigate: head right as far as you can, then go up as far as you can, then head right again. This will take us to the first lit area at the top-right. As you enter that room, keep walking as far right as possible—there’s another hidden path.

Room layout reference: first lit area at the top-right, plus a hidden path further right
Write-up notes

Once you reach the first dead end on the hidden path, head downward until you can’t go any further, then go right again. Keep repeating this pattern and you’ll eventually reach the “room of many rooms.” The doors have markings above them B ⬆️ A

The “room of many rooms” area after following the hidden corridor pattern
Write-up notes

We start the “maze of many rooms” by entering any door, then following the previously noted reversed Konami code.

A B ➡️ ⬅️ ➡️ ⬅️ ⬇️ ⬇️ ⬆️ ⬆️

If you choose the wrong door, you’ll end up back in the neighborhood. Before stepping through a door, you can verify it’s the correct one by right-clicking the tile just before the doorway and selecting Inspect. If the door’s div looks like the following (or mentions datacentermaze-city), it’s the wrong door — don’t enter. This is useful for getting oriented in each room, which makes it easier once you reach the direction steps in the Konami code.

Write-up notes

The correct door’s <div> tag will include both the current room reference and the room you’re headed to. There are 11 rooms total and the final one is labeled snowlab.

Write-up notes

After successfully navigating the rooms, you’ll find yourself in the Snow Lab with Frosty’s Snowglobe Machine. Exit the room to shut down the machine and collect your Snow Crystal.

Item
Snow Crystal

A crystal powered by holiday magic. Legend has it this crystal manifests its owner's most-desired gift during the holiday season. In Frosty's case, that gift was the power to cover the city in snow forever. Hm... didn't something similar happen in a movie once?
